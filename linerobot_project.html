    • LINE FOLLOWING ROBOT

    An autonomous robot with attitude

    Our final project for 2.678, MIT's Electronics for Mechanical Systems class, is to build a, autonomous line following robot that navigates a maze without previous knowledge of the layout. With this challenge, we were given an Arduino, an infrared sensor array, and a robot frame. The maze consisted of a 1 inch wide black line that the robots were to sense and follow. However, obstacles were designed into the track, such as Figure 8 loops, garbled lines, blank spots, and more. Robot successfulness was judged by speed, accuracy, and ability to get through the whole track.

    We were able complete the source in 1:37, ranking in the top half of high-scoring robots. However, in future iterations of the robot, I would have included a more efficient search algorithm that utilized path prediction rather than the inefficient 180 scan used for this version.
